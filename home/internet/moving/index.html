Is Frontier becoming Verizon?

Frontier is now a Verizon company, bringing better value and more choices to millions more customers across the country. By integrating Frontier's complementary pure-play fiber network with Verizon's industry-leading Fios and mobility assets, the company now has an expanded reach of almost 30 million fiber passings across 31 states and Washington, D.C. With the greater availability of premium home internet and mobility services, customers have exclusive access to new services, savings and value they can't get anywhere else. This expansion includes plan offerings for fiber optic home internet, fiber optic business internet, landline phones, and Fios TV.

See if you're eligible for fiber home internet by checking availability.

I'm a Frontier customer and I'm moving. What actions do I need to take?

For existing Frontier customers, you can check availability to see what internet options are available at your new address. If you remain in the Frontier footprint, you will continue to manage your move through your Frontier account.

Can I move my current Verizon Home Internet service to a new address?

Verizon can help you move your home internet service to a new address. Verizon makes it easy for you to stay connected while you start your new adventure. To find out if Verizon offers home internet at your new address, enter your address or check out our coverage map to see what other services may be available. For existing Fios customers, sign into your Fios account to get started. For 5G Home, Verizon Home Internet Lite and LTE Home Internet customers, sign in here to see if service is available at your new address.

If you have more questions about moving your Verizon Home Internet, check out our movers FAQ page.

What are the steps to moving my home internet?

Moving your home internet to a new address is easy. Follow these steps to ensure you have a smooth transition:

  • 1. Step 1 - Qualify service for new address
  • 2. Step 2 - Schedule your move
  • 3. Step 3 - Setup at your new address

If you have any questions, please visit our home internet moving guide, support services or reach out to our customer service by chatting with our virtual assistant or giving us a call at 1.800.837.4966

What internet is available at my new address?

To see which Verizon home internet service is available at your new address, check availability. You can also check the coverage map to find out what other services may be available where you live.

The home internet service available at your new address will depend on where you're located. Fios provides 100% fiber-optic internet with supercharged speeds and is widely available in metro and suburban areas of the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic like New York City, Boston, and Philadelphia. 5G Home Internet is now in more and more places around the country and is fast, reliable home internet so you can game, stream and connect the way you want.*

If you find that Verizon Home Internet is not available at your new address, you may still be able to purchase Verizon Wi-Fi Backup. Which can quickly get you back online or if your internet is down. Find out more about Verizon Wi-Fi Backup to keep your home connected to our award winning wireless network.

*5G Home and Verizon Home Internet Lite services provided through cellular network; speeds vary due to factors affecting cellular networks.

When should I set up Verizon Home Internet at my new home?

You should set up your Verizon Home Internet as soon as you move to your new home, and no later than 30 days from your initial order date. Your move order is only active for 30 days, so if that lapses, your registration may be canceled and you may need to place a new move order. The sooner you set up your home internet, the sooner you can start streaming, gaming, and browsing the web.

I'm moving and Verizon internet is available at my new address. Do I need to pay an installation fee?

You might not need to pay a set up fee depending on your plan and location. For 5G Home service, you can set up the equipment on your own, at no cost. In some areas, 5G Home professional assistance may be available for an additional charge.

If you're transferring Fios internet to a new home, there may be an installation or setup charge when your new service is installed. Log into your account and let us know what you will be moving to begin transferring your internet service.

What internet speed do I need when I'm moving to a new home?

Your ideal internet speed will depend on how you are using the internet. If your household has multiple devices connected to stream TV shows, work from home, take video calls, or play video games, then faster internet speeds might be the best option for you.

Verizon Fios

  • Fios 300: With download speeds up to 300 Mbps, you can easily stream (including 4K video), game and make video calls on multiple devices at once, and get a 3-year price-lock guarantee.
  • Fios 500: Download full-length movies in minutes or immerse yourself in online virtual reality games and get a 4-year price-lock guarantee.
  • Fios 1 Gig and 2 Gig: Enjoy even lower latency and less lag, with enough bandwidth for any activity and lots of connected devices and get a 5-year price-lock guarantee.

Verizon 5G Home Internet

  • 5G Home: Get reliable download speeds capable of 1080p HD video streaming and a 3-year price lock guarantee.
  • 5G Home Plus: Enjoy unlimited premium data with faster download speeds and a 4-year price-lock guarantee, so you don’t have to worry about unexpected rate hikes. Unlimited data usage is subject to Verizon's Customer Agreement.
  • 5G Home Ultimate: Offering our fastest fixed wireless speeds and a 5-year price lock guarantee, you can stream shows and movies in brilliant Ultra HD 4K resolution

Verizon Home Internet Lite

  • VHI Lite: Unlimited* data at speeds up to 25 Mbps downloads (typical 10-25 Mbps) with a 3-year price lock guarantee. After 150 GB of data use, speed will be reduced to 10 Mbps download for the remainder of the billing month.
  • VHI Lite Extra: Get unlimited* data at speeds up to 25 Mbps downloads (typical 10-25 Mbps) with a 4 year-price lock guarantee. After 200 GB of data use, speed will be reduced to 10 Mbps download for the remainder of the billing month.

*Unlimited data usage is subject to Verizon's Customer Agreement.

When I'm moving, can I keep my current plan and equipment?

Keeping your internet plan and equipment depends on which service and equipment are compatible with your new address.

If the service you currently have isn't offered at your new address, you will need to switch to the service that is available and get new equipment that is compatible with that service. Enter your new address to find out if you can bring your current plan and equipment to your new home.

I'm moving to a new apartment. How do I get internet service there?

If you're moving to a new apartment, the internet service offered will depend on many factors. Some apartments let you purchase your own internet service, while others have internet services provided within the building. When moving to a condo or an apartment, check with your landlord first to see if they offer Verizon Home Internet Services.
